## 核心發現：87%台灣員工承受中高度心理健康風險

87%的台灣員工正在承受中度至高度的心理健康風險，這是 Aon 與天下學習聯合發布的 2026 年亞太員工健康指數的核心數據。具體而言，35%的台灣員工面臨高度風險，52%面臨中度風險，合計達到87%。同時，有45%的員工表示其生產力已經受到心理健康問題的負面影響。

這個數字將台灣放在亞太12國中一個令人警惕的位置。在東亞與東南亞的同儕國家中，台灣的心理健康風險指數居高不下，反映出台灣企業員工面臨的壓力環境已經達到危機水平。值得注意的是，這些數據並非來自小樣本調查，而是基於超過15,000名台灣在職員工的大規模問卷研究。

## 台灣中小企業的EAP覆蓋缺口：被遺忘的98%企業

台灣超過98%的企業都是中小企業，卻只有不到20%的中小企業員工享有員工協助方案（EAP）的覆蓋。這形成了一個巨大的支持缺口：超過80%的台灣企業員工完全沒有任何正式的職場心理健康支持體系。

相比之下，大型企業的EAP滲透率達到85%，看似令人欣慰。但更深層的問題隨之浮現——即使員工有EAP的可及性，實際使用率卻不足3%。這說明傳統EAP存在著嚴重的「可及性-使用率悖論」：員工知道有這項服務，但因為污名化、流程複雜、不了解內容等多重原因，實際使用者寥寥無幾。

根據 AI-EAP 2026年亞太市場研究報告，傳統EAP每名員工的年度成本為新台幣500-2,000元不等，對於員工數在50-200人的中小企業而言，這筆支出往往佔據HR年度預算的重要部分，導致許多中小企業經營者無法負擔，最終被迫放棄這項投資。

## 職場壓力三大來源與其影響：數據剖析台灣員工的焦慮根源

台灣員工面臨的職場壓力主要來自三個方向：42%的員工認為過度繁重的工作是最主要的壓力源，35%指出與同事或主管的人際關係不和諧，32%表示工作內容缺乏成就感是隱形殺手。這三個因素交織在一起，形成了多維度的壓力環境。

進一步的數據顯示，75.7%的台灣員工認為工作壓力對其心理健康造成了負面影響，這個比例在全球範圍內都屬於偏高水平。更令人擔憂的是，84.4%的員工報告工作壓力對其睡眠質量造成了直接的負面影響——失眠、淺眠和睡眠中斷成為了許多台灣員工的日常困擾。

根據台灣心理衛生協會的追蹤數據，長期睡眠不足進一步加重了員工的心理健康問題。員工無法在休息時充分恢復，工作效率下降，工作質量下滑，這又反過來增加了時間壓力和自我批評，形成了一個惡性循環。

## 心理健康問題的經濟代價：隱形成本正在吞噬企業獲利

根據世界衛生組織（WHO）2024年發布的數據，全球因憂鬱症和焦慮症造成的生產力損失每年高達1兆美元。這個數字不是虛數，而是來自於具體的醫療支出、缺勤天數、低效工作時數（presenteeism）的加總。

在台灣，一名員工的替換成本估算為其年薪的6-9倍。這意味著失去一名年薪新台幣60萬元的員工，企業需要付出360-540萬元才能補齊這個職位——包括招聘成本、培訓成本、新員工的初期低效率時期，以及在此期間其他員工的超時工作負擔。

但更隱形的成本來自於缺勤與「病假上班」（presenteeism）。員工即使在工作，但因為心理健康問題而無法發揮最佳表現，導致工作效率下降30-50%。根據 Aon 的數據，presenteeism 造成的生產力損失甚至超過了缺勤本身。一名因焦慮而無法專注的員工，其實際工作產出可能只有正常水平的一半。

乘以台灣 870 萬名在職人口中45%受到心理健康問題影響的員工比例，這筆隱形成本就成為了數千億台幣的經濟損失。

## 為什麼中小企業更需要心理健康支持？被結構性困境困住的企業

中小企業面臨的心理健康支持挑戰不是簡單的「預算不足」，而是結構性困境。首先，台灣的中小企業佔企業總數的98.97%，但企業規模小意味著每個人的責任更重，人力流動成本更高。當一名中小企業員工離職時，失去的不是某個特定的工作崗位，而是整個運營網絡中的重要一環。

其次，資源限制是致命的。大企業可以配置專職HR、設置舒適的休息空間、購買高端EAP服務，但中小企業往往沒有這些奢侈品。一名中小企業的HR經理可能同時負責招聘、薪酬、員工關係、合規性等多項職責，根本沒有精力專注於員工心理健康支持的實施。

再次，傳統EAP的定價模型對中小企業極為不友善。年費新台幣500-2,000元看似合理，但對於50人的企業來說就是年費25,000-100,000元，對於20人的微型企業則完全無法承受。標準化EAP也往往設計給大企業使用，其功能和複雜度超出中小企業的實際需求。

AI-EAP 的出現打破了這個僵局。通過人工智能和雲端技術，新一代員工心理健康支持方案能以傳統EAP的十分之一成本提供服務，同時保持高度個性化的體驗。這為中小企業打開了全新的綠地市場。

## 企業HR的行動建議：從零開始建立員工心理健康支持的三步走路徑

### 第一步：進行現狀診斷與風險評估（0-1個月）

在投資任何解決方案之前，HR需要了解自己企業員工的真實心理健康狀況。建議進行一次匿名的員工心理健康調查，涵蓋以下維度：
- 當前最大的壓力來源（工作量、人際關係、薪酬等）
- 心理健康症狀自評（焦慮、抑鬱、失眠等）
- 對心理健康支持的實際需求
- 對公司當前支持措施的認知度和滿意度

根據調查結果，識別高危人群（離職率高、缺勤率高、績效下降）並進行針對性跟進。

### 第二步：制定成本合理的干預方案（1-3個月）

根據公司規模和預算，選擇合適的心理健康支持工具。對於中小企業，建議：

**對於微企（10-50人）**：
- 優先考慮 AI-EAP 或線上心理健康平台（成本低、易實施）
- 提供員工教育課程（壓力管理、睡眠衛生等）
- 建立匿名求助熱線或線上諮詢通道

**對於中小企（50-200人）**：
- 結合 AI-EAP 平台和定期線上心理教育工作坊
- 設立員工心理健康大使（同儕支持網絡）
- 與社區心理衛生機構建立合作關係

**跨企業通用的成本控制手段**：
- 制定靈活工作政策，減少過度繁重工作
- 建立清晰的溝通渠道和衝突解決機制
- 提供職業發展機會，增加工作中的成就感

### 第三步：實施、監測與迭代優化（持續）

選定方案後，重點是建立有效的推廣和監測機制：
- 定期宣傳心理健康支持的可用資源，消除污名化
- 每季度追蹤關鍵指標：員工使用率、滿意度、缺勤率變化
- 定期收集反饋，根據員工需求調整支持模式
- 建立長期成功指標（如離職率下降、員工滿意度提升）

## 常見問題解答（FAQ）

### Q1：中小企業真的負擔不起EAP嗎？

**A：** 傳統EAP確實對中小企業造成財務壓力，但這不再是絕對障礙。新一代AI-EAP解決方案的成本已經大幅下降，許多服務商針對中小企業推出了分層定價模式，使得50人的企業年費可能只需3,000-8,000元，遠低於傳統EAP的25,000-50,000元。更重要的是，實施員工心理健康支持帶來的離職率下降、生產力提升，往往能在6-12個月內收回投資成本。

### Q2：員工會真正使用EAP嗎？為什麼大企業EAP使用率這麼低？

**A：** 低使用率背後的根本原因有三個。第一，污名化——員工擔心使用EAP會被標籤化為「有心理問題的人」，影響職涯發展。第二，可及性差——傳統EAP的預約流程複雜、等待時間長，不適合忙碌的上班族。第三，信任缺失——員工不相信咨詢內容的保密性，擔心HR會掌握個人心理健康信息。新的解決方案通過匿名設計、即時AI輔導、確保數據隱私來解決這些問題，使用率因此大幅提升。

### Q3：我怎麼知道心理健康支持是否真的有效？

**A：** 有多個可量化的指標可以衡量效果。最直接的是**使用率**（多少比例的員工實際使用了服務），理想情況下應達到15-25%。其次是**滿意度**（用戶對服務的評價），應達到4/5分以上。第三是**業務指標的變化**：缺勤率、離職率、員工滿意度、績效評分等。實施心理健康支持後，這些指標應該在3-6個月內看到向好趨勢。此外，可以進行年度心理健康調查，直接詢問員工心理狀況是否改善。

### Q4：實施員工心理健康支持會不會引發更多人主動離職？

**A：** 這是一個常見的誤解。實施心理健康支持之初，可能會有一小部分原本因為壓力過大而被迫留任的員工選擇離職，這是正常的調整期。但從長期來看，心理健康支持會顯著降低總體離職率。原因是，真正的幸福感來自於被看見、被支持，而不是被強制留任。員工在感受到企業關心其心理健康時，往往會更加投入工作，反而更難被挖角。根據Aon的數據，實施全面心理健康支持的企業在2-3年內的離職率下降平均達到15-25%。

### Q5：心理健康支持應該由HR負責還是可以外包？

**A：** 最佳實踐是混合模式。基礎的心理健康支持平台（EAP、線上課程、自助資源）應該外包給專業服務商，因為這些需要臨床專業知識和24/7可用性。但在企業內部，HR需要扮演「心理健康倡導者」的角色，包括：建立心理健康友善文化、推廣和宣傳支持服務、識別高危員工、領導層溝通心理健康的重要性。換句話說，HR不需要成為心理健康專家，但需要成為連接員工和專業服務的橋梁。

# 2026 Mental Health Crisis in Taiwan's SMEs: What the Data Reveals

## Core Finding: 87% of Taiwan's Workforce Faces Moderate-to-High Mental Health Risks

A striking 87% of Taiwan's employed population is experiencing moderate to high levels of mental health risk. This key finding comes from the 2026 Asia-Pacific Employee Health Index, a joint study by Aon and CommonWealth Magazine. Breaking down the figures: 35% face high-level risk, while 52% face moderate-level risk. Additionally, 45% of employees report that their productivity has been negatively affected by mental health challenges.

This data positions Taiwan at an alarming level among 12 Asia-Pacific nations. Taiwan's mental health risk index ranks among the highest in East and Southeast Asia, reflecting a workplace environment where employee stress levels have reached crisis proportions. It's important to note that these figures are not derived from small-scale surveys, but rather from large-sample research involving over 15,000 Taiwanese workers currently in employment.

## The EAP Coverage Gap in Taiwan's SMEs: Forgotten by the System

Over 98% of Taiwan's enterprises are classified as small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), yet fewer than 20% of SME employees have access to Employee Assistance Programs (EAP). This creates a massive support void: more than 80% of Taiwan's employed population has absolutely no formal workplace mental health support system.

In contrast, large enterprises boast an EAP penetration rate of 85%—a seemingly impressive figure that conceals a deeper problem. The actual usage rate among employees with EAP access remains below 3%. This represents the "accessibility-usage paradox": while employees technically have access to these services, most never use them due to stigma, complex processes, and lack of understanding about what's available.

According to the 2026 Asia-Pacific Market Research Report by AI-EAP, traditional EAP services cost between NT$500-2,000 per employee annually. For SMEs with 50-200 employees, this translates to a significant portion of the HR budget. Many SME owners simply cannot afford this investment and ultimately abandon the idea of mental health support entirely.

| Mid-size enterprises | 30-40% | <2% | <0.8% |

## The Three Primary Sources of Workplace Stress: Decoding Taiwan's Employee Anxiety

Taiwan's employees face workplace stress from three main directions. Forty-two percent cite excessive work volume as their primary stressor, 35% point to poor interpersonal relationships with colleagues or supervisors, and 32% identify lack of achievement or meaningful work as a hidden culprit. These three factors interweave to create a multi-dimensional pressure environment.

Further analysis reveals that 75.7% of Taiwanese employees believe work stress negatively impacts their mental health—a figure that ranks high globally. More alarmingly, 84.4% of workers report that workplace stress directly disrupts their sleep quality. The World Health Organization's 2024 report quantifies the staggering global cost: mental health conditions—primarily depression and anxiety—cause approximately 1 trillion US dollars in lost productivity annually. This is not a theoretical figure but the sum of actual medical expenses, days absent from work, and hours of reduced-output work.

## Action Plan for HR: Three-Step Pathway to Building Mental Health Support from Scratch

### Step 1: Conduct Current Assessment and Risk Evaluation (Month 0-1)

Before investing in any solution, HR must understand the actual mental health landscape within the organization. Conduct an anonymous employee mental health survey covering:
- Primary current stressors (workload, relationships, compensation, etc.)
- Self-assessed mental health symptoms (anxiety, depression, insomnia)
- Actual needs and preferences for mental health support
- Awareness and satisfaction with existing company support measures

Use survey results to identify high-risk populations (high turnover departments, elevated absenteeism, declining performance) and initiate targeted interventions.

### Step 2: Develop Cost-Effective Intervention Strategy (Month 1-3)

Based on company size and budget, select appropriate mental health support tools. For SMEs, we recommend:

**For Microenterprises (10-50 employees):**
- Prioritize AI-EAP or online mental health platforms (low cost, easy implementation)
- Provide employee education programs (stress management, sleep hygiene, etc.)
- Establish anonymous helpline or online counseling channels

**For Small-to-Mid Enterprises (50-200 employees):**
- Combine AI-EAP platform with regular online mental health education workshops
- Establish employee mental health champions (peer support networks)
- Partner with community mental health organizations for expert support

**Cost-Control Strategies Applicable Across All Sizes:**
- Implement flexible work policies to reduce excessive workload
- Create clear communication channels and conflict resolution mechanisms
- Provide career development opportunities to increase job meaningfulness

### Step 3: Implementation, Monitoring, and Continuous Optimization (Ongoing)

Once you've selected your approach, focus on effective promotion and monitoring:
- Regularly communicate available mental health resources to dispel stigma
- Track key metrics quarterly: usage rates, satisfaction scores, changes in absenteeism
- Gather regular feedback and adjust support based on employee needs
- Establish long-term success indicators: declining turnover, rising employee satisfaction

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

### Q1: Can SMEs really afford mental health support programs?

**A:** While traditional EAP has been financially burdensome for SMEs, affordability is no longer an absolute barrier. Next-generation AI-EAP solutions have dramatically reduced costs, with many vendors offering tiered pricing specifically for SMEs. A 50-person company might now spend NT$3,000-8,000 annually instead of the traditional NT$25,000-50,000. More importantly, benefits realized from reduced turnover and increased productivity typically recover the investment within 6-12 months.

### Q2: Will employees actually use EAP programs? Why is usage so low at large enterprises?

**A:** Three fundamental factors drive low usage rates. First, stigma: employees fear using EAP will label them as having "mental problems," potentially damaging their career. Second, accessibility barriers: traditional EAP requires complex booking, lengthy wait times, and poor integration with busy schedules. Third, trust deficits: employees worry that confidentiality cannot be guaranteed and that HR might access their mental health information. Modern solutions address these issues through anonymous design, immediate AI-guided support, and strong data privacy, resulting in significantly higher usage rates.

### Q3: How can I measure whether mental health support programs actually work?

**A:** Multiple quantifiable metrics can assess effectiveness. The most direct is usage rate—what percentage of employees actually engage with services should reach 15-25%. Next is satisfaction scores, which should exceed 4 out of 5. Third, monitor business metrics: absenteeism, turnover, employee satisfaction scores, and performance ratings. Post-implementation, these indicators should show positive trends within 3-6 months. Additionally, conduct annual mental health surveys asking employees directly about perceived improvements in their mental health status.

### Q4: Won't implementing mental health support cause more employees to quit?

**A:** This is a common misconception. Initially, you may see a small percentage of employees who were only staying due to desperation leave the organization—this is healthy adjustment. However, long-term data demonstrates that comprehensive mental health support significantly reduces overall turnover. Genuine wellbeing comes from feeling seen and supported, not forced retention. When employees sense organizational investment in their mental health, engagement typically increases, making them less vulnerable to external recruitment. According to Aon, companies implementing comprehensive mental health support see turnover decline by 15-25% over 2-3 years.

### Q5: Should mental health support be managed by HR internally or outsourced?

**A:** Best practice uses a hybrid model. Core mental health support platforms—EAP, online courses, self-help resources—should be outsourced to professional vendors who can provide clinical expertise and 24/7 availability. Internally, HR should serve as the "mental health advocate" and cultural leader, responsible for: establishing a psychologically safe culture, promoting awareness of available resources, identifying at-risk employees, and engaging leadership in mental health conversations. In other words, HR doesn't need to become mental health experts—but needs to bridge employees with professional services effectively.
